What are Stocks???

Have you checked the news recently and seen headlines like NVIDIA IS FLYING ON THE MARKET. Do you ever wonder what that means or how it works? You’re on the right site then! We’re here to breakdown and explain what stocks are and how they work, potentially even getting you into investing!


How Does the Stock Market Work?

So What is a Stock?

A stock represents a share of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you own a piece of that business.

How Does it Work: If the company grows and becomes more valuable, the value of your share (the stock price) usually increases. There is also risk associated with stocks because if the company losses value, your stock may also lose value. Companies may also pay dividends (a portion of profits shared with stock holders).


Why Do People Buy Stocks: Historically the stock market has been on a upwards trend with an increase of 7–10% per year on average after inflation over the last century(what’s even better is that this is compound growth!). Since 1996, the S&P 500 (basket of 500 top companies in the US) has increased by 790%, a much higher return than what you’d get just getting compound interest at your bank. With stocks, you can grow your wealth faster to help reach goals like buying a house, retiring, and more. You are also able to create passive income with stocks through dividends.

Where Do You Buy Stocks: Stocks are traded on exchanges like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) or NASDAQ, where buyers and sellers set prices based on supply and demand. You as an individual are able to participate in these exchanges through brokerage firms like Charles Schwab, Fidelity, Vanguard, Robinhood, and more.
